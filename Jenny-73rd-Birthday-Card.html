CELEBRATING YOU TODAY, AND ALWAYS

A little poem, just for you

Seventy-three candles, lit just for you —
Jenny: steady, gentle, and true.
Minnesota mornings all know your name,
your kitchen, your garden, your heart just the same.

Not long ago, a knee said "not yet,"
but you never once settled for regret.
Each careful step forward, a little more strong —
that's the quiet kind of brave you've carried along.

You hold his hand through the fog and the years,
through moments forgotten and moments still clear.
Dementia may take what it takes, we all know —
but it hasn't once dimmed the love that you show.

Three children you raised, now grown steady and tall,
still finding their way back to you, through it all.
Retired now, with hours finally your own —
for coffee, for grandkids, for slow afternoons.

And somewhere in Europe, a café saves a seat
for the woman who's earned every mile off her feet.
Cobblestones, castles, the wind off the sea —
someday soon, Jenny, that's just where you'll be.

So today, let the candles and flowers all say
what we've always known, in our own quiet way:
you are steady, you are loved, you're still somebody's north —
happy 73rd birthday, Jenny. Here's to all you're worth.

With love, today and always
